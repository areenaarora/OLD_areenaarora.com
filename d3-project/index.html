Exploring NYC payroll data with D3

New York City's payroll dataset is one I'm familiar with and have written in detail about it too. For this project however, I wanted to see how much easier D3 makes my life (hint: It did not).

I can now say I know (ish) JavaScript a bit and thanks to this exercise, I understand D3's syntax better. All these charts were made possible thanks to The D3 Graph Gallery!

In reporting on this data previously, I learned that some agencies spend way more than others on overtime pay— for various reasons.

This first chart here shows the same! Here, I've plotted overtime spending by agencies in 2021.

New York Police Department spent close to $500 million in just overtime across all its employees. The next highest was the Fire Department at a little under $300 million.

For this second chart, I've plotted number of years of service on the x-axis and overtime hours worked on the y-axis.

The color of the bubbles corresponds to the agency the employee works for. For this chart, I've only plotted employees who made over $200,000 in total pay in 2021 from the top three agencies that spend the most on overtime.

The chart that didn't quite work:

This chart was meant to show gross salary and overtime pay. The x-axis shows gross base salary and y-axis shows overtime pay. Some employees in the $90,000 to $130,000 gross salary range made over $100,000 in just overtime.
