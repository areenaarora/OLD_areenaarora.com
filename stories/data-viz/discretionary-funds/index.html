New York City Council allocated over $371 million in discretionary funding in 2021. Another $5 million worth of projects were categorized as pending for approval, as of April 22.

The top five biggest recepients include three non-profit law firms. Together they received $16.6 million for a program called New York Immigrant Family Unity Project. The program is meant to provide legal support to detained immigrants facing deportation who cannot afford an attorney.

Broken down by boroughs, Manhattan-based initiatives got nearly $180 million, which is almost half the total pool.

For the largest recepient — City University of New York — the money funded 22 programs. These include adult literacy initiatives, immigrant support services and a program for veterans' development, among others.

The biggest chunk of the funding for CUNY goes to a scholarship fund called the Peter F. Vallone Academic Scholarship. The nearly $16 million fund, cleared in 2021, was designated for eligible students' scholarships during the 2019-2020 academic year.

CUNY was the top recepient of the funding pool in 2017 and 2020 too. Their initiatives largely focus on adult literacy, research and food access.

Here are some highlights from the database:

  • The Peter F. Vallone Academic Scholarship fund is the highest funded program. It received over $48 million between 2019-2021.

  • The funding pool stood at about $413 millin in 2009, the first year for which data is available. In 2021, the total funding was about $371 million.
