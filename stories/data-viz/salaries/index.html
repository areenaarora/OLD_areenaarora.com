70 ex-NYC employees made more in overtime, on average, than everyone else

What does that mean?

Data from NYC city payroll shows how three employees NYC Housin Authority and the Department of Health/Mental Hygiene made over $220,000 in overtime pay.

Between 2017-2021, 1556 out of over 142,000 city employees worked more hours in overtime than regular hours. They represent 49 of the 150 agencies on city's payroll. This is a data exercise into what their payroll data can tell us, without any journalistic interviews.

The data for this story is sourced from NYC Open Data.

Of these 1556 employees, 432 are from the Police Department, followed by 184 from the Department of Children's Services and 150 from Parks & Recreation Department.

For 581 employees, their regular work hours were listed to be below zero in the dataset. The data dictionary doesn't offer any details into how that could happen. For now on, we'll therefore focus mostly on the ones that have positive regular hours worked noted.
